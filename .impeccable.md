## Design Context

### Users
University students who want to share and read real interview experiences from their peers. They use the platform after going through recruiting cycles — either to give back by documenting their experience, or to prepare by reading others'. Context: anxious pre-interview prep, or reflective post-interview sharing. Tech-savvy, mobile and desktop, often late-night browsing.

### Brand Personality
**Sharp, resourceful, peer-driven.** The interface should feel like a well-engineered tool made by students, for students — not a corporate product and absolutely not AI-generated. It should radiate technical competence, robustness, and trust.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Industrial-tech with warmth. Think: a well-designed developer tool crossed with a community platform. Dense but readable. Functional but alive.
- **Theme**: Dark mode. Deep surfaces with emerald-tinted neutrals. Depth through surface lightness, not shadows.
- **Typography**: Display — Schibsted Grotesk (angular, confident, Nordic authority). Body — Figtree (warm humanist sans, excellent readability on dark backgrounds).
- **Color**: Emerald green accent (OKLCH hue ~155). Tinted neutrals toward green. No cyan-on-dark, no purple gradients, no neon accents.
- **Motion**: Smooth scroll, staggered entrance reveals, exponential easing. No bounce. No elastic. Motion should feel inevitable, not decorative.
- **Anti-references**: Generic Glassdoor clones, AI slop dashboards with gradient text, anything with cards-on-cards or hero metric layouts. No glassmorphism.

### Design Principles
1. **Density with clarity** — Pack information tightly but maintain clear hierarchy through typography weight and spatial rhythm, not borders or cards.
2. **Earned trust** — Real company logos, structured data, community signals (votes, comments) visible upfront. No empty promises.
3. **Speed as personality** — Interactions feel instant. Optimistic UI. Smooth scroll. The platform feels snappy because students are impatient.
4. **Community-native** — This isn't a corporate product. It should feel like something built by peers, with contribution and sharing as first-class experiences.
5. **Technical honesty** — Show real data, real difficulty ratings, real outcomes. No sugarcoating. The aesthetic should match: robust, direct, unflinching.
